Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income, Poverty and Age.

The states on the lower end of median household incomes, when compared to those with higher median incomes, show a tendacy for higher poverty rates. This correlates to a higher rate of the states' population without healthcare.

It is clear the lower income states also face higher obesity and smoking rates.

A state's economic profile has more correlation with health risks than age does.
